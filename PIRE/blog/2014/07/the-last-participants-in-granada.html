The Last Participants in Granada

By KYRA LEIGH KRASS on July 2, 2014 8:43 AM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

Hello again! I can´t believe it´s already July! I´m trying not to think about the fact that this week is my last week of testing participants, but sadly it´s the truth. I´ve now tested 30 participants. I have several more scheduled to come in this week, and I´m hoping they all show up. Unfortunately my one participant yesterday did not. I sent out reminder emails to the rest of my participants for this week though, so I think that they will all come in.

In the past week, Paige and I went to Sevilla and Lagos. We met a ton of new people at our hostel who were very friendly. We went out with several of them, and we even met up with some of our friends that we met in Morocco. While in Sevilla, I saw the Alcazar and the Cathedral. I also had some of the most delicious tapas that I've had so far here in Spain. We were only in Sevilla from Wednesday night until Friday afternoon, but we still managed to explore the city and go on a walking tour.

After our brief time in Sevilla, we headed to Lagos. Lagos is an absolutely beautiful beach town. The sandy beaches were a sight to see when we arrived. We got to walk along the beach for a little before we went out to dinner with the group. When we finally went to dinner, Paige and I met some new friends from around the US. We went to a restaurant called NahNahBah which had the best burger I've ever had. It had the usually toppings of cheese, lettuce, and tomato, but it also had bacon and pineapple. The combination of toppings was perfect. It turns out the owner of the restaurant is from Massachusetts. We weren't the only ones who loved the burgers either. This restaurant was voted one of the top 50 burger joints in the world. The group got to go out together after dinner as well. We went to a few different bars around town, and then we went to a club to go dancing. It was all very fun.

On Saturday, I went on a sailboat cruise with part of the group who was in Lagos. We got to take small speedboats into the caves to explore. They were all very beautiful. After the cave exploration, we went onto the bigger sailboat and got to jump into the ocean. It was freezing, but very refreshing and fun. We jumped in the water several times before we headed to the beach to take in the sun. I went in the ocean for a little bit, and we all had a great time relaxing in the sand. After leaving the beach and cleaning up, the group headed to the "end of the world" to see the most southwestern point in Portugal that people once thought was the end of the world. It was beautiful, and we got to watch the sunset despite some clouds. The group then went out to dinner again and then to another bar in town.

On Sunday, Paige and I went kayaking. That was so exciting. I had never been before, and I was very eager to try it. We paddled through the caves again and then stopped on a beach to go snorkeling. I've done that before, but it´s been many years. I forgot how difficult it was to remember only to breathe out of your mouth. We got to see some fish and some sea anemone. Our one friend has a GoPro camera so he took a bunch of underwater pictures. I'm eager to see how they turned out. After our excursion we headed out to lunch, at NahNahBah again, and then back to the hotel to head home. We took the bus to Sevilla and then caught another bus back here to Granada.

I don't officially know what my plans are for this weekend and next week, but I'm hoping to go to Madrid and Barcelona in the last few days. It would be a shame to leave Spain before I saw the two largest cities. I'll keep you all posted about my last adventures. Happy July everyone!
