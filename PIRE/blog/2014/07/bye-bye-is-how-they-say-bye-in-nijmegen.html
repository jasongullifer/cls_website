Bye Bye (Is How They Say "Bye" In Nijmegen)

By DANIEL JEFFREY MAGERMAN on July 27, 2014 10:19 AM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

There is time for just a few brief words before I pack up my laptop and put it into storage as Mary Beth, Mindy and I embark on the last leg of our PIRE adventures -- two weeks in Spain!


From State College, to Palenque, to Nijmegen, this research experience has been a whirlwind of new languages, ideas, challenges, faces, places, and friends. After our first lab meeting with John Lipski in August 2013, the notion of fieldwork was just beginning to materialize as a possibility. John introduced us to the inner workings of Lengua and to Palenque, but actually flying to Colombia to pursue linguistics seemed a bit farfetched.


Fast forward to May 2014: Mary Beth, Mindy and I flew to Cartagena. We immersed ourselves in the languages and the cultures. In Palenque, we learned about much more than a creole. We encountered an entirely different, yet not so foreign way of life and made some incredible friends along the way. Then, in June, we arrived in the Netherlands. With the help of Dr. Pieter Muysken and numerous other grad students and professors at Radboud University, we took our raw data and processed it into meaningful, sharable results. Besides making progress with our research, we had the chance to really live life day-to-day in a new city. Also, I had the chance to befriend and to play ultimate with Radboud's team, the BFrisbee2's, a great bunch of people. Even when I'm in beautiful Spain and back in State College continuing work on my research, I will sorely miss Nijmegen.


And so, the time has come to extend a heartfelt thank you to everyone who made this summer possible. This wasn't just a overseas research crunch-the-numbers kind of experience; this was unforgettable. Thank you to everyone at Penn State who works hard to make PIRE happen. Thank you to Dr. Muysken for being such a patient teacher and for being so excited on our behalves. Thank you to John for everything, particularly helping us to love Palenque as much as he does. Thank you to all the kind, generous, (mostly Dutch) people I've met here, especially the BFrisbee2's. Thank you to Jenny who worked on a separate PIRE project in Nijmegen. I wish she could have stayed longer! And finally, thank you to Mindy and Mary Beth. Without you guys, this summer would've had a lot less laughter and far fewer adventures -- it wouldn't have been the same!













Gracias, bedankt, and thanks!
