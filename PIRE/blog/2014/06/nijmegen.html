Nijmegen

By MELINDA PAIGE DENNIS on June 17, 2014 2:37 PM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

I can't believe how lucky I am. I am living in the Netherlands, in the biggest apartment I've ever lived in, stepping out onto our 11th floor balcony every morning and watching the Dutch do what they do best: biking. Simultaneously, I am learning more than I could have ever imagined about more than I ever thought I would.

From the first day here, I was blown away by the beauty of this country. It is wonderful in all its modesty. While I love visiting the city center and seeing the old square lined with brick buildings, decked out in red shutters and topped in stepped gable (classic Dutch architecture), I really enjoy wandering on the residential streets where each house almost always has a sweet little garden out front which have the most perfect flowers (what girl doesn't love flowers). I don't know what it is about the flora here but even the wildflowers are more beautiful than anything I would be able to grow back home. Maybe it's because 50% of the country is less than one meter above sea level.

What a transition it was to come here after spending two weeks in Colombia. Third World to First World, extreme humidity to chilly and breezy, fruits and starches to fried food, Spanish to Dutch/French/English/German and many more. I never expected that I would get involved in linguistics (in fact I didn't even know what linguistics is a few years ago) but how could I not have, when I am blown away every time I am greeted in public in more than one language. Hallo, bonjour, hoi! Take your pick. 

As far as my project goes, I am currently in the process of coding the data I collected in Colombia. I have a great time going through the recordings and remembering all the people I met there. The coding process so far is time consuming but interesting since I can already get an idea of how my results will turn out. Aside from this independent work, Daniel, Mary Beth, and I have attended a number of CLS talks and even a dissertation defense. The Radboud professors have been so nice for willingly getting in contact with us and inviting us to presentations and or meet us in person and even for setting up a chance for Daniel, Mary Beth, and myself to present our projects at their CLS meeting next week.
