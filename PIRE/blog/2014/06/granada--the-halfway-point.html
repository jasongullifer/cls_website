Granada- The Halfway Point

By KYRA LEIGH KRASS on June 11, 2014 12:03 PM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

Hello all. I've reached the halfway point here in Granada, and I have to say, I'm in denial. I actually cannot believe after all the work that went into preparing to leave, that I'm already halfway through. So far I've tested 19 participants which, luckily, is about ¾ of the participants that I need. So far I had one more no show, but at least this time the participant told me before I left our apartment to go to the lab. I'm definitely in a groove with testing participants, and I basically have it down to a science. Gone are the nerves. All I have to think about during the experiment is will the participant have good data, and not, where do I put this electrode. I think that I am conversing with the participants in Spanish with more ease. I find that I don't worry as much about whether I'm 100% grammatically correct because chances are the participant knows what I mean and will tell me how to correct what I said.

Since my last blog post, Paige and I went to Córdoba. It was a beautiful city. We got to tour the Mezquita/Catedral, and I was also able to explore the Alcazar and the gardens. I took many pictures there. Here's one from Saturday. We were originally planning to stay the entire weekend, but we were able to see all that we wanted to see by Saturday afternoon, so we came back to Granada early and enjoyed some relaxation time. Working at the lab and also fitting in travel has been a bit exhausting, but I'm not complaining! There are so many places to explore still, and I'm eager to fit them all in before July rolls around.  

This weekend coming up, Paige and I are headed to Morocco. I am very excited. This is the trip that I think we have been most looking forward to. It'll be our first time in Africa, and I think that it will be amazing. It will be unlike anything we've ever seen before, and bonus- we'll get to ride a camel. We received our itinerary Monday, and we will be spending time in Tangier, Chefchaouen, and Asilah. Hopefully we'll be able to sample some tea and also some couscous. I'm looking forward to this weekend so much, and it will be a big reward after this long, busy week.

Tomorrow, Paige and I will be giving presentations to the lab on our PIRE research. I am glad that I had a presentation prepared already from one of our small lab meetings. I will let you all know how that goes, but I'm not too nervous about it. I would be if it had to be in Spanish, though. I don't think I have enough linguistics/science vocabulary in Spanish to speak about my research for very long. I think that's all for now. More on Africa to come in the next blog post.
