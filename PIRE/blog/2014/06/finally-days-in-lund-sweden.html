Finally days in Lund, Sweden

By JULIA ROSE HOTCHNER on June 26, 2014 5:50 AM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

My last  week in Lund has finally come. It has been so easy to get lost out here and disconnect from world ( causing my lack of blogging ;) ) The people here live a calm lifestyle which at first I didn't understand. I was confused when I would get on public transportation and it was silent  or  see many people out to eat by themselves  ( and not pretending to be busy on their phones ) Back at Penn State I'm studying speech pathology. Every course that I've taken the teacher always reminders us that the first step is diagnosing a speech disorder is deciding if the patient has a "speech disorder or a speech difference." If your patient is pronouncing "dawg" instead of "dog" it might be because they have a problem with their speech or  they simply live in New York City and have formed an accent. As my time passed in Lund I began to apply this lesson to my life. Many Swedish people aren't going to make small talk on the bus with you not because they don't like you but because many Swedish people are shy and don't want to bother a complete stranger. Also eating  alone  doesn't make you anti-social or lack friends, some people simply want to some alone time that happens to be in public. I could go on and on about all the cultural difference I've experienced while aboard.  I think what I'll take away from this experience is that nothing is wrong with your own culture or the new culture you are in. It's just different and seeing these differences is a beautiful thing. 

 

 

 

 

This past weekend was Midsommar! This a holiday celebrating the first day of summer. It was a fun day filled strawberries, flower crowns and dancing around the maypole. My roommates and I went to a traditional maypole dance where we tried to follow the locals in their dances. Later that night we had a big dinner at home. Once we were free from our food coma we went out to pick flowers for our flower crowns. The Swedish people believe that on Midsommars night you should place seven different flowers from seven different fields under your pillow. That night you will dream of the man you're meant to be with.  Unfortunately for me I dreamt of my mother! But besides that it was a lovely day and a successful Midsommar.

 

 

Sunday, my departing day, will be bittersweet. I am meeting my mother in Paris. We will spend 3 days there and then head to Ireland to visit our family. As excited as I am for the trip I will miss the friends I've made here. My roommates have been so wonderful. Everyone came from somewhere different . There was Louise, the local Swedish girl who would grow almost all her food and was a "extreme recycleist. " but would never force  her extreme lifestyle onto you. She is a great listener and never made a promise she couldn't keep. There was Anna, a PhD student from German. I've never seen a girl  eat so much and remain so thin. She spoke her mind and also took everyone's opinion into account. She made everyone feel welcome no matter where she went.  Lastly there was Kelly, a girl from the Atlanta. When I first arrived I thought we were have so much in common. As we got to talking we found out we had such different views and lifestyles. Almost every night we would eat dinner t

ogether. I think that's what I'll miss the most. Our talks over dinner would last hours discussing anything from the perfect time to have coffee to political views. The dinner table was just one giant melting pot of culture. No one was right and no one was wrong, just honest.

 



Friday I will finish my testing! I will end with 20 participants. Although it doesn't seem like a whole lot I am thrilled to end with this many people.  Battling against finals and specific  requirements made recruiting difficult at times.  I was informed that an abstract  I submitted to the Mental Lexicon in Queen lakes Canada was accepted ! Lots to look forward to in the coming school year. I know I am first finish my Pire experience. It was a challenging yet rewarding experience .  Best wishes to all other students! Take it all in, it goes fast :)
