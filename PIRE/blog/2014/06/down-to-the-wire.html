Down to the Wire

By KYRA LEIGH KRASS on June 24, 2014 8:51 AM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

Hello, readers. It´s been a little while since my last blog post. Things have been good here in Granada. I just finished up my 26th participant, so I have officially reached my goal number for participants! I´m very excited about that. I have around 9 more people scheduled though, so I´ll be busy next week finishing up my experiment. I´m sad to say my time in Spain is running out. In just about two weeks I´ll be boarding my plane home to Pennsylvania.

The past two weeks have been very eventful. Two weekends ago, Paige and I were in Morocco. It was quite the experience. It was a long journey to get there, but the reward was great. It was so amazing to see a new continent. The culture was so different from Spain's. The food was all very good. We ate a lot of bread, meat, and salads. Everything was very flavorful. The most popular spice was curry. While we were there, we got to shop in several different cities, tour Chefchaouen and Asilah, and ride camels. The camel ride was my favorite. It was so much fun. Here's a picture of me right after the camel stood up.

This past week was Corpus Christi, so CIMCYC was closed Wednesday through Friday. Paige and I were happy to have some time off. On Wednesday we saw the parade which featured people running around with large papier mâché-type heads. Thursday was the actual holiday, so everything in the city was closed. We spent the day inside, but we went to the fair that night. It was fun. There was music, dancing, food, games, carnival rides, and horses- tons of horses. We spent several hours there on Thursday, and we returned on Saturday to enjoy the merriment again. We sampled a chato which was a small shot of very sweet wine that is typical for the fair. It was very strong, but we were told we had to try it to truly get into the spirit of the fair. The fair was much more crowded on Thursday than Saturday, but we enjoyed ourselves both days nonetheless.

On Sunday, Paige and I went to the beach again. This time we went to Salobreña. It was pretty cloudy, but we still managed to get a little sunburn. I'm thankful I was wearing sunscreen because I can only imagine what I would have looked like if I hadn't been wearing it. We were told that the next day was the celebration of San Juan and that there would be many fires built on the beach to celebrate, but unfortunately Paige and I had to work and couldn't go. It seemed like fun, but we were able to go to a free, outdoor concert last night instead to celebrate. The singer, whose name we didn't catch, was very talented. She sang, played guitar, and played piano.   

Neither Paige nor I have any participants scheduled for the rest of the week because we are planning another trip. Tomorrow and Thursday we are hoping to spend some time in Sevilla. Then, Friday through Sunday we are going to be in Lagos, Portugal. We are going on the trip with the same company that took us to Morocco, so we are very excited. It should be a blast. We'll get to spend some time on the beach and maybe do some kayaking or surfing.

Happy Summer, everyone!
