Wir lieben Herzogenriedpark!

By ABIGAIL PEARSON MASSARO on June 23, 2014 12:15 PM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

I often feel that, after a certain amount of time in a city, one tends to forget what's great about that city. When I first came to Mannheim, everything about it was new enough that I was always excited to go exploring and there seemed to always be new things that I hadn't seen yet. Sometime around one or two weeks ago, I stopped feeling like there were new things that I hadn't seen and I was more content to stay in my apartment and do work rather than try to find new things to do or even different, local places to sit with my computer and get things done.

Yesterday Molly and I decided though that we would try to do a bit more sightseeing in our last few weeks and we went to Herzogenriedpark, the local park that lies halfway between our two apartments. I had wanted to go to the park before, as it was only about 15 minutes away from my apartment, but the entry fee always deterred me. Why, I thought, should they charge me to go into a green space? But once we got there, we saw it was in fact much better; Herzogenriedpark is an extensive set of grounds that has everything from a tremendous rose garden, to a sizable jungle gym with a climbing rope web that was nearly two stories tall. There was entry into a pool area with slides, as well as an area for minigolf, and even a petting zoo! (One of the goats was nice enough to smile for me when I took his picture.)

We ended up spending nearly four hours in the park, spending a large of that time eating blueberry cake and listening to Blasmusik (traditional German polka) with over a hundred senior citizens and playing in the big giant rope web. The weather was tremendous and the park has numerous lounge chairs that are available to park-goers to sit in and sunbathe by the fountains or gardens; after Molly left, I ended up grabbing a chair and sat out read my new copy of "Die Tribute von Panem" (the German version of "The Hunger Games").

I wish that I had realized earlier how great the park was and that it was absolutely worth the cost of entry for all of the great things that they have to do there--especially on a Sunday. In Germany, as with many other European countries, the vast majority of things are closed on Sunday and there's not much to do except stay at home or spend time outside. The streets are usually fairly empty and I always imagined that people stayed in with their families. I was wrong--it seemed like everyone in the city had gone to the park, just as we had. It was really enjoyable to see people out and being with their families in a green space and taking advantage of the great things that Mannheim has to offer. Molly and I have plans to go back a few more times before the summer's over--and I'm so excited!
