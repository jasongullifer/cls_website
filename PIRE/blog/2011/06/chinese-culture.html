Chinese Culture

By NICOLE PAIGE WASILUS on June 14, 2011 2:01 AM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

It's now week four and I've had a lot of exposure to Chinese culture and everyday life here. I thought I'd make this blog post for the future PIRE student conducting research at Beijing Normal University (BNU). I've noticed many differences and similarities between here and the US that I think would be helpful for a future student to know before he/she goes abroad. Although I'm sure they will still experience a culture shock, hopefully this can lessen the blow. I'll list what I've learned here:

-Most people in China do not know English. Luckily, the graduate students in my lab do. It is important to try to practice Chinese with the graduate students (although you may be tempted to stick with English) so that you can survive outside of the lab.

-They have most of the same brands that we have. They have some snacks that we have (like ice cream, chips, etc.) and some of the same toiletry brands (Dove, Pantene, etc.).

-You cannot drink the water out of the tap here. Always ask for bottled water, or Yī píng shuǐ  瓶水. Also, Chinese people do not like cold drinks so don't be surprised if you get warm soy milk with breakfast.

-There are a few American restaurants and fast food places around BNU. There is a Pizza Hut, Subway, Mcdonald's, and KFC not too far away if you are craving bread or American food.

-It seems that rules of the road in China are arbitrary. Be careful when crossing the street, even when you have the light to cross!

-If you are not Asian, you will get many confusing looks (especially if you are a girl). Come in with the expectation that you may be stared at or that people may want to take your picture.

-Spitting is common here. It is not considered rude or in bad taste.

-Taxi drivers do not know English. Always have destinations written out in Chinese.

-Hotels must have a special license to accept foreigners as guests. If you get rejected from places, don't take it personally.

-There are no dryers so you have to hang your clothes to dry them. There are places to get your laundry washed and folded on campus for a few dollars.

-At BNU, most students come into the lab on weekends and stay late during the week. I thought this was because they were so hard working (which they generally are) but it is really because their dorms are so crowded and are shared with 6 people or more without air conditioning.

-I have yet to find wireless while here. You must be plugged in with a Ethernet cord to receive internet.

-The subway station is very easy to figure out. They have lit up signs in English and Chinese, as well as announcements before each stop. Try not to go during rush hour though, it can get pretty crowded!

-You have to be aggressive in line here or someone will take your spot. If you don't appear to be ready to order or pay, someone else will jump in front of you who is.

-Many people wear the same exact outfit the next day. Also, everybody dresses nice. Girls are mostly in dresses and heels for class every day.
-When you pass a friend walking, many people say "你吃了吗?" It means: "Have you eaten yet?" It is just a common greeting and it doesn't mean that they want to go to lunch with you.
-There are huge lazy susans at traditional Chinese restaurants and all of the food is shared.

 


Most importantly, keep an open mind! Some changes you will get used to over time and some you have to deal with, though you may never understand them. Try not to constantly compare US and China, and just take it for what it is! Best of luck!
