Initial Impressions and Travel Tips

By NATHANIEL JAMES HOLLISTER on May 3, 2011 6:26 AM | 4 Comments | No TrackBacks

            The last few days have been quite a unique experience to say the least. After leaving the States on Saturday for Granada, I embarked on what turned out to be a 27-hour trip to actually arrive at the apartment which I am staying in. However, I was lucky enough to be traveling with both Jason and Lauren, so when our flight from Madrid to Granada was cancelled on Sunday morning, at least I had people to talk to until our rebooking later that afternoon; although I don't think that I was much of a conversationalist, falling asleep on a table and all. The flight cancellation was a very new experience for me. Initially, I was very nervous because this was my first time traveling without my family, so I wasn't too sure what to do. But in the end everything worked itself out and the three of us made it to Granada. If I have one piece of advice for those unfamiliar with traveling alone and have not done so yet, however, it would be to make sure that you have the contact information of your host mentor ready in case something happens. Even if you're just making a simple change, letting your mentor know is always better than not doing so.
   Read on for more...

Another piece of advice that I would give is to bring very little work that is due in the immediate future. I was in a very peculiar situation where I had to do a paper while I was traveling because I could not finish it before leaving and let me tell you, that was an awful experience. Mental note: work and planes do not mix well. Better mental note: work and little sleep do not mix well. Luckily, I have since completed and submitted the paper that I had, but I would not suggest that anyone ever do that in the future. Ever.
    After arriving in Granada, I soon began to realize that it was not all that different from American cities. Sure, everything looks different and the people around you have a different native tongue, but ultimately, it's just a city like any other one. And honestly, I'm glad that I had that realization when coming here because I was very nervous before leaving, having never really been abroad before. So I guess that my final piece of advice would be for anyone who is nervous about traveling abroad (for the first time or not) is the following: You are probably making it out to be much worse than it actually is and in the end everything is going to fine.
    I am sure that I will have my fair share of experiences to share with everyone. And when I get started with the research portion of my stay, I will be sharing that with everyone as well. For now though, everyone who is preparing to leave for their foreign sites, good luck and I look forward to reading your posts!
