The Importance of Staying Flexible

By PATRICK JOSEPH CLARK on May 25, 2011 2:21 AM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

As more time passes, our time here in Beijing becomes less and less overtly unfamiliar.  Sure, Ben's beard, my checkered flag backpack, and most recently, Nicole's mere presence still draw many double-takes from the locals.  But every day, I learn more and more.  Due to a complicated IRB application protocol, I am still not cleared to perform research here at Beijing Normal University.  A predicament for sure, but my time has certainly not been wasted.  Every day, I have been reading as many papers as I can get my hands on regarding age of acquisition and proficiency, and their connection to bilingual research. 

I have met many valuable resources here, as well!  Yesterday, Ben and I were lucky enough to have lunch with Dr. Youyi Liu, a colleague of Dr. Li's from his time at Richmond and now a faculty member here at BNU.  Dr. Liu offered much information regarding our research and life here in Beijing, as well as a possible opportunity to play badmintion in a tournament here at BNU!  Wish team USA luck!  We're definitely going to need it.  

Also, many of the members of my new "team" of Professor Shu's students are graduate students.  As such, they are preparing their research papers for submission to academic journals.  One student who has been especially helpful from our first day here, Lijuan Zou, recently had a manuscript on structural plasticity submitted and successfully accepted by Cortex, a journal devoted to neurologic study!  Congrats, Lijuan!  Lijuan has allowed me to read her manuscripts in return for my grammatical revisions, and I have learned a lot from her about both her research topics and fMRI techniques. Here is Lijuan and I at the Great Wall!

 

 

 

 

 

 

Despite minor setbacks, my time here at BNU has already been AWESOME.  Every day is unpredictable.  Many thanks to Lijuan, Wuhan, and Chao for their help.  I hope all the other PIRE sites are enjoying their time as much as I am.
