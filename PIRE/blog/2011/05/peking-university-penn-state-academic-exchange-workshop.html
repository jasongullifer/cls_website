Peking University & Penn State Academic Exchange Workshop

By Benjamin Zinszer on May 20, 2011 6:20 PM | 4 Comments | No TrackBacks
On May 18th and 19th, I attended a small conference at Peking University (Beijing, China) to celebrate the renewal of a Statement of Understanding between PSU and PKU and facilitate collaboration between the schools. The workshop I participated in was a fascinating fusion of computational linguistics and public health called "Smart Health and Wellbeing." There are several researchers at both institutions studying natural language processing to:
  1. enhance communication in health services
  2. provide improved cross-linguistic access to health information for speakers of English and Chinese
  3. and design more intelligent bio-monitoring systems that can advise doctors and patients
  4. and others!
My presentation discussed the lifetime concerns of language development for bilinguals undergoing first language attrition and a SOM model used for studying this phenomenon:

AMELIA JANE DIETRICH | May 30, 2011 10:48 AM | Reply

Ben! How is your Chinese so far?

Benjamin Zinszer | May 31, 2011 6:12 AM | Reply

It's not great, but I'm getting a little better each day. Strangely, I've found speaking to be quite a lot easier than understanding. I can speak telegraphically about some extremely narrow topic, and even if my pronunciation is horrible, people figure out what I mean. But I don't understand a thing most people say to me, even when it's words I already know in a very obvious context (such as a cashier asking about change).

I've actually thought a lot about speech segmentation, and I believe I'm getting considerably better at that. As that improves, I'm sure I'll learn more of what words are supposed to sound like, instead of depending on my nonsense mental representations of them. Then let the vocabulary spurt begin!
