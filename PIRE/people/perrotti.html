Bio:
I am an undergraduate student at the Pennsylvania State University. I will graduate in May 2012 with two Bachelor of Arts degrees, one in Spanish and one in Italian, and a minor in Linguistics. I am in the Schreyer Honors College as a Paterno Fellow, an innovative program that promotes academic excellence.

In 2009, I worked as a research assistant in a German Second Language Acquisition Lab using eye-tracking equipment to study case-marking. I also worked as a research and teaching assistant in the English Department with the Sony Corporation to record and analyze the use of the e-Reader in a university setting. In the summer of 2010, I studied abroad in Todi, Italy to enhance my education in Italian. I have recently received a PIRE undergraduate award to travel to Granada, Spain during the summer of 2011, in order to pursue eye-tracking research investigating grammatical gender in Spanish, with specific attention to cognates.

At Penn State, I work with the Special Olympics and Sue Paterno to organize and promote the Beaver Stadium Run, a 5k that ends on the 50 yard line of Beaver Stadium. I give campus tours and host university-wide events as a Lion Ambassador. I serve on the College of the Liberal Arts Academic Integrity committee, and I dance in the Penn State International Dance Ensemble. I am the Digital Media Chairperson and Philanthropy Chairperson for the Liberal Arts Undergraduate Council. I have eclectic interests; from performance, to academia, to graphic design, and I always put my heart in all that I do!
