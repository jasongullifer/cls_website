Core Faculty (PSU) - Dr. Nola Stephens

Position: Visiting Assistant Professor of Linguistics

Institution: Pennsylvania State University, USA

Email: stephens@psu.edu

CV: Nola Stephens CV

Personal page: Nola Stephens web page

Lab/Group: N/A

Bio:
Nola Stephens received her Ph.D. in Linguistics from Stanford University in 2010. She teaches undergraduate courses in linguistics, and her primary research interests are in first language acquisition, information structure, argument structure, lexical semantics, and psycholinguistics.

Representative Publications:
Stephens, N. (In prep) Givenness and pronominality in preschooler's dative constructions.

Stephens, N. (In prep) The effects of givenness on children's locative constructions.

Hall-Lew, L. & Stephens, N. (In Press) Country talk. Journal of English Linguistics.

Stephens, N. (2006) Norwegian 'when'-clauses. In Miriam Butt & Tracy Halloway King (Eds.), Proceedings of the LFG06 Conference (pp. 474-484). Stanford, CA: CSLI Publications.

Stephens, N. (2006) Agentivity and the virtual reflexive construction. In Benjamin Lyngfelt & Torgraim Solstad (Eds.), Demoting the agent: Passive, middle and other voice phenomena (pp. 275-300). Amsterdam: John Benjamins.
