Bio:
I am a fifth year graduate student in cognitive psychology, co-advised by Drs. Judith F. Kroll and Janet van Hell. I am interested in using both behavioral and neuroscientific methods in the study of bilinguals as a means of examining cognitive processing. Some of my recent projects have investigated bilingual advantages in foreign language vocabulary learning, working memory, and executive function. More recently, I have become interested in two unique groups of bilinguals: bilinguals who know signed languages and simultaneous interpreters. The language experiences of both of these bilingual groups seem to confer differential cognitive benefits than those of general bilingualism. Currently, I am conducting a series of projects aimed at investigating the underlying mechanisms of language learning, with an ultimate goal of identifying which aspects of language learning are shared with other types of learning (e.g., implicit learning, procedural learning, etc.), and which aspects are distinct. In addition to my work, I enjoy watching excellent television shows (such as The Wire, Veronica Mars, and Arrested Development), baking cupcakes and other desserts, and playing narrative video games (such as Portal and L.A. Noire).
