Core Faculty (PSU) - Marianna Nadeu

Position: Assistant Professor of Spanish and Linguistics

Institution: Pennsylvania State University, USA

Email: nadeu@psu.edu

CV: View as PDF

Bio:

I am an assistant professor of Spanish and Linguistics in the Department of Spanish, Italian and Portuguese at Penn State University. I received my Ph.D. in Romance Linguistics at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2013. My areas of research are phonetics and laboratory phonology, and I am especially interested in the acoustic and articulatory effects of prosody at the segmental level in Romance languages, as well as in mechanisms of speech enhancement and reduction more generally. My research interests also include language variation and change, historical linguistics, bilingualism, contact linguistics, and the expression of paralinguistic meaning through intonation.

Representative Publications:

  • Mora, J. C. & Nadeu, M. (2012). L2 effects on the perception and production of a native vowel contrast in early bilinguals. International Journal of Bilingualism, 16, 484-499.

  • Hualde, J. I. & Nadeu, M. (2011). Lenition and phonemic overlap in Rome Italian. Phonetica, 68, 215-242.

  • Hualde, J. I., Simonet, M., & Nadeu, M. (2011). Consonant lenition and phonological recategorization. Laboratory Phonology, 2, 301-329.

  • Nadeu, M. & Prieto, P. (2011). Pitch range, gestural information, and perceived politeness in Catalan. Journal of Pragmatics, 43, 841-854.
