Foreign Partners - Marianne Gullberg

Position: Professor Psycholinguistics and Director of the Humanities Lab

Institution: Lund University, Sweden

Email: Marianne.Gullberg@ling.lu.se

Lab/Group: Lund Humanities Lab

Bio:

My research is multidisciplinary and multimodal. I study adult second language (L2) acquisition and use, L2/bilingual processing, and gesture production and comprehension in acquisition using a range of techniques including eye-tracking, ERPs, and more traditional behavioral methods. I am interested in the human language learning capacity, in how the representations of a new language develop and the co-exist with the representations of other languages in the minds and brains of individuals, and in the multimodal expression of language in real time. My work focuses on the earliest stages of adult L2 acquisition as well as on the advanced or bilingual stage.

I examine effects of lexical semantics in learning and processing at the sentence level, cross-linguistic (bi-directional) influences; discourse, and code-switching. Gesture analysis is used as a tool to probe these issues further, but gestures are also studied in a perspective of crossmodal information processing. Between 2003-2008, I headed the group The Dynamics of Multilingual Processing at the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics, the Netherlands (with Prof. P. Indefrey). I am also co-founder of the Nijmegen Gesture Centre (with Prof. A. Özyurek).
