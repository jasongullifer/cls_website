Bio:

I am a first year student at Penn State pursuing a dual title Ph.D. degree in Psychology and Language Sciences, working with Dr. Michele Diaz and Dr. Judith Kroll. I got my master degree in psychology at Beijing Normal University, China in 2014. My current research interests include language and cognition on different groups of people, especially on bilinguals and aged people. I will be travelling to Granada to conduct with Teresa Bajo at the University of Granada, Spain from May-June, 2015. My PIRE project will focus on the cognitive consequences of retired interpreters. I will be looking at whether the cognitive benefits as a simultaneous interpreters will disappear after they retired.
