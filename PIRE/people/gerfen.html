Core Faculty (PSU) - Dr. Chip Gerfen

Position: Associate Professor of Linguistics and Spanish Linguistics

Institution: Pennsylvania State University, USA

Email: gerfen@psu.edu

CV: Gerfen CV

Lab/Group: Chip's Phonology Lab

Bio:
I am a phonologist interested, broadly speaking, in laboratory phonology and in experimental work in general on both bilingual and monolingual language processing.

Representative Publications:
2010     Shelton, M., C. Gerfen, & N. Gutiérrez Palma . How Spanish phonotactics informs psycholinguistic models of speech production. . In K. Arregi, Z. Fagyal, S. Montrul, & A. Tremblay (Eds.), Romance Linguistics 2008. Amsterdam/Philadelphia: John Benjamins. 249-264.

2010     Weiss, D., Gerfen, C. and Mitchel, A. (2010). Colliding cues in word segmentation: the role of cue strength and general cognitive processes. Language and Cognitive Processes 25. 402-422.

2009     Weiss, D.J., Gerfen, C. & Mitchel, A.D. Speech segmentation in a simulated bilingual environment: A challenge for statistical learning? Language Learning and Development 5, 30-49.

2008     Kroll, J. F., Gerfen, C., & Dussias, P. E. Laboratory designs and paradigms in psycholinguistics. In L. Wei and M. Moyer (Eds.), The Blackwell guide to research methods in bilingualism and multilingualism (pp. 108-131). Cambridge, MA: Blackwell.

2006     Gerfen, C., & W. Rizzo. Phonological variability in the laboratory: Word naming in bidialectal speakers. In J.P. Montreuil (ed.), New Analyses in Romance Linguistics. John Benjamins. 83-96.

2005     Gerfen, C., & K. Baker. Production and perception of laryngealized vowels in Coatzospan Mixtec. Journal of Phonetics. 33-3. 311-334.

1999     Gerfen, C. Phonology and Phonetics in Coatzospan Mixtec. Studies in Natural Language and Linguistic Theory. Dordrecht: Kluwer Academic Press. 304 pages.
