The Young Scholar Speaker Series (YSSS) is an annual event founded and organized by the graduate students in the Center for Language Science (CLS) with the support of the CLS Director and Associate Co-Directors. The goal of the YSSS is to further integrate the graduate students into the CLS community, and to provide important training for the future careers of the CLS students. Each year, a graduate student committee, composed of student representatives from such diverse academic backgrounds as the Department of Communication Sciences & Disorders, the Department of Germanic & Slavic Languages, the Program in Linguistics, the Department of Psychology, and the Department of Spanish, Italian & Portuguese.

Each year, the YSSS graduate student committee calls for nominations for the invited speaker to come to Penn State. In selecting this speaker, all CLS graduate students are invited to vote on the nominated candidates to select the invited speaker. This speaker will be asked to participate in a series that includes individual appointments with students and faculty, workshops led by the speaker, poster presentations of graduate student research, as well as a lecture by the invited researcher to be presented at the CLS weekly speaker series.

In selecting an invited speaker, the YSSS graduate student organizing committee accepts nominations from all participating CLS graduate students. The criteria for nomination are:

  • PhD completed in the last 5-10 years
  • Nominee has made a significant contribution to his/her area of expertise
  • Research interests in diverse subfields of language science in order to reflect the CLS community

The 2013 YSSS invited speaker was Dr. Susanne Gahl from the University of California at Berkeley. Her research lecture, presented at the CLS weekly speaker series was entitled, "Conversational speech and psycholinguistics."
