Three weeks down!

By NICHOLAS ADAM ANDEREGG on May 30, 2014 5:23 AM | No Comments | No TrackBacks
So among other things that have happened in the past two weeks, I've learned that I'm really bad at updating blogs and I think that's just a fact of life that we're all going to have to accept! (Seriously, this New Post tab has been open in my browser for a week and a half!)

Anyway, onto things that actually happened!

The past week has been pretty productive and we've tested quite a few participants. Of course, like in any subject pool you have to draw from, there have been those that have fallen asleep or failed to show up at all, but in general, more people have been tested than not! In the previous week, I was not yet able to begin testing because of issues with the testing software, but now that that has been resolved, things are going pretty well. People are actually learning my experiment's pseudowords in the training portion of the task, which is great.

I've been attempting to pick up Mandarin while I'm here (because who would pass up an opportunity for immersion language learning), and I think if I can get past the first hurdle of learning the sounds of the language, I'll be able to make some progress. Hypothetically, spoken Chinese shouldn't be extremely difficult for an English speaker to learn as the grammar is much simpler, the words are generally much shorter, and in general, it requires no conjugations. However, the sound system is so wildly different that it's hard to even pick out syllables. Luckily, Chinese only has about 423 distinct syllables, so I figure I'll just memorize the phonetic representations of all of them and go from there (and a large portion are extremely similar to English). I feel like I'm making things more complicated than it has to be, but pointing at everything and looking confused is getting exhausting!

Last week, I got a tour of all of the psych facilities at this university and the coolest thing by far was their mock scanner. Unlike the mock scanner at Penn State, which really just simulates the bore that participants will be in, the mock scanner here looks exactly like the real thing, including the warning about metal objects on the door. 
I'm pretty sure I almost had a heart attack when I walked through the door, saw the mock scanner, and wondered why we were just allowed to walk in their with our cell phones, etc.

Overall, it's been a pretty productive two weeks. I'll try to update this thing more often from now on!
