Granada Week 1!

By KYRA LEIGH KRASS on May 20, 2014 8:31 AM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

Greetings from Granada! It has been a long but eventful week since leaving the States. The flights to get here went as smoothly as they could have. I was very lucky to have been awake not only to watch the sun rise, but also to look out the window and see the very first glimpse of Europe as we flew over the coast of Portugal. Getting to look down on a new continent was very exciting.

So far, here in Spain, I have felt very welcomed. Manolo gave me the grand tour of the apartment and has been very helpful in pointing both Paige and I in the right direction to find the bus stop, the grocery store, good places to eat, etc. He brought us into the lab this past Thursday and we were able to meet Teresa and many other people who work in the lab. Everyone was very welcoming and helped us whenever we needed assistance with anything which is good because we seem to have a lot of questions. The facility itself is gorgeous inside, but it has an even more amazing view of the city. We were able to see the rooms where we will be working for the next 7 weeks, and we were also able to attend the weekly lab meeting.

On the cultural front, I have slowly been getting used to living here in Granada. The jet lag that was around for the first few days has finally disappeared. I'm not quite used to taking a siesta and eating dinner at 10 pm, but I'm sure I will be in due time. I have taken the opportunity to walk around the city as much as possible this week. So far Paige and I have seen beautiful views of the Alhambra, the Cathedral, the Sierra Nevada, and many other wonderful parts of the city. We're planning on taking tours of all Granada's landmarks and taking as many pictures as we can. We even booked our ticket to see the Alhambra. Much to our surprise, tickets were sold out until June.

I'm looking forward to testing participants. I started sending out emails yesterday to recruit participants, and I already have 8 people scheduled. It's very exciting. Hopefully I continue to find willing participants. Granada is, after all, teeming with monolingual Spanish speakers.

I hope all is well in State College, PA.
