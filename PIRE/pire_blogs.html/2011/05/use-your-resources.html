Use your resources!

By NICOLE PAIGE WASILUS on May 2, 2011 4:31 PM | No Comments | No TrackBacks

Throughout the semester, I've tried to expose myself to the Chinese culture as much as possible. I enrolled in Chinese 197- All About China (one credit course), joined the Chinese Connection Club, bought an audio book to learn Chinese and became a conversation partner through Global Programs. All of these experiences, I believe, will help me adjust to the huge culture shock I'll be faced with this summer. I recommend to any future PIRE student to use the resources that are available to them at Penn State. Audit a class, talk to international students from the country you will travel to, try to figure out the cultural differences, etc.

 Specifically, for the future PIRE student going to China, I would like to go into some detail about the things I've tried:

-Chinese 197 brought in guest speakers from all disciplines to talk about China- the movie industry there, censorship issues, etc. The class switched off weekly between guest lectures and learning the language. Instead of trying to learn the characters, we learned 'pinyin' which is much simpler! The class was taught by Chunyuan Di. They are working on revamping the course for next semester to make it more useful for students traveling to China. Check out some of the other Chinese courses Penn State has to offer here: Chinese Courses at Penn State

-The Chinese Connection Club is a student organization that meets weekly (every Tuesday at 6:00 in Willard building). Some of the activities I participated in were: making tangyuan (i.e. sweet dumpling), practicing calligraphy, celebrating the Chinese New Year, etc. I met a lot of international students who were very helpful to me.

-The audio book I purchased comes with CDs that you can upload to your Itunes and a book to follow. I mostly listened to it on my phone between classes and walking around campus. I would recommend it (and be willing to let anyone borrow it!) Check it out here: Living Language

-One of my favorite things I have done this semester was signing up for the Global Programs conversation partner program. Here is a link to the website: Global Programs! I was set up with a graduate student named Dong and we met weekly to talk. He helped me learn a few basic words and I helped him practice the phonemes that were giving him trouble in English . We started to try a new restaurant every week, since he was intimidated about ordering in American restaurants. I hope to be able to meet up with him while I'm in China!

Hope this helps! 

Zài jiàn 再见
