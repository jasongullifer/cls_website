Touring China

By PATRICK JOSEPH CLARK on June 14, 2011 12:55 AM | 1 Comment | No TrackBacks
Weekdays here at Beijing Normal University have a fairly consistent pattern - largely filled with doing what I can to adapt my research plans to accommodate another small setback.  Fortunately, with the help of Ben and my new colleagues, I believe we have turned a slightly inclement situation into a positive one!  Borrowing some of Ben's literature and knowledge of fNIRS, I believe I have developed a suitable plan to use the valuable time and resources I have at my fingertips here at BNU to compile some valuable language-related data.  I look forward to learning more about the advantages of fNIRS, particularly in regards to its leniency when it comes to the subject's physical movement while still collecting data. This week, we also heard from Dr. Marius Peelen, from the University of Trento, Italy.  He discussed the neural mechanisms underlying seemingly effortless familiar object detection in convoluted scenes.  The weekends, however, have yet come anywhere close to ordinary, especially for a small-town Penn-Stater from rural PA!  Last weekend, Ben, Nicole, and myself went to The Temple of Heaven with two colleagues from Taomei's lab. This has been on my Beijing bucket list from the time I first heard I would be heading to China.

The Temple of Heaven turned out to be not just a temple, but a series of buildings, walkways, structures, and gardens, each of which with its own unique purpose.  Our guides, Vivian and Taoran provided us with cool tidbits of historical information about each feature.  Much of the complex was for the emperor alone, but despite having tons of incredible architecture all to yourself, there's a lot of work that goes into being the spiritual and political leader of a dynasty.  Here's a picture of Taoran, Ben and myself goofing around in a small tunnel exiting one of the gardens.

 

 

To keep the weekend trend going, Ben, Nicole, and I packed up a change of clothes and some toiletries and piled into a train destined for Shanghai last Friday.  We bought tickets about a week in advance for fear of not getting a sleeper car.  We departed from Beijing around 9:30 PM and arrived in Shanghai at 7:30 the following morning.  First we went on a bicycle tour of some of the sites.  This turned out to be an excellent choice (well played, Nicole), as it saved our feet and took us to parts of Shanghai we hadn't heard about at all on the internet.  Most notably, we ended up in a small neighborhood of shops and restaurants, each with a unique trade item for sale.  These items were slightly more quality (and less difficult to get a good price on) than the items at the Pearl Market in Beijing.  It was all very fun.  Then we met up with a friend of Nicole's from PSU.  Dong was incredible nice. His parents us treated to a great dinner, found us an even greater hotel, and even drove us to The Bund, a popular lookout spot with a tremendous view of the Shanghai skyline.  It was raining, but we made it work.  On Saturday, we started the day with a trip to the Yuyuan Gardens, a beautiful maze of ponds, caves, and bridges, built by the son of a high-ranking Chinese official to please his father.  It was honestly one of the most serene places I've ever been, even with the other tourists around.  I wish I could've been there before it was such an attraction... We followed the gardens with a trip to the more modern side of Shanghai - across the river.  It was very different from the opposite side - with skyscrapers and modern architecture.  We owe a big "thank you" to Dong and his family.  Hopefully we'll see him at PSU! Last night, we got into our bunks again for the return trip to Beijing.  I sit here now very tired, but excited for yet another adventure - this time to Oslo in a few hours.  By this time tomorrow, I'll be adjusting to one more time change in Norway!  Updates to come!
